\# Property Of



A psychological narrative game about identity, compliance, and chemical surrender.



\*\*Content Warning:\*\* This game contains depictions of emotional, physical  and sexual abuse, 

coercive control, medical trauma, psychosis. 

It explores themes of Stockholm syndrome, dissociation, and loss of autonomy.



\---



\## About



You are Ingram Svalander. Twenty-seven years old. Swedish-Dutch. Engineering student 

dropout. Schizoaffective.



You live in a two-bedroom apartment in Chicago with Rémy, your partner of six years. 

He is an anesthesiologist. He administers your medication. He chooses your clothes. 

He holds your key.



The game follows a single day — morning to night — as you perform domestic rituals 

while navigating the erosion of your selfhood. There are choices, but the narrative 

is linear. The choices are not about where you go, but how you feel getting there.



Some mechanics:

\- \*\*Awareness\*\* — Understanding the nature of your captivity

\- \*\*Sanity\*\* — Coherence of self; your grip on who you are

\- \*\*Energy\*\* — Physical capacity; depleted by resistance, restored by compliance
